Stirfry Recipe

Description

This is a stirfry recipe that I use a lot. It doesn't take a lot of time or preparation and it tastes pretty good. I learned most of it from my mom.

Ingredients

  • 2 Eggs
  • Some cooked rice (a little over 2 cups of cooked rice works, but honestly any amount works
  • Broccoli
  • Soy sauce, or oyster sauce
  • Vinegar
  • Chili oil (like laoganma)
  • Cooked shrimp
  • Garlic
  • Garlic powder (optional, but I really like garlic)
  • Cooking oil (like olive oil)
  • Green onion

Steps

  1. Put cooking oil in a pan and set it to medium heat.
  2. Cut the broccoli however you like it.
  3. Mince the garlic.
  4. Put the garlic in the pan. The pan should be hot enough that the garlic starts sizzling.
  5. After a few seconds, add the broccoli. The broccoli should also start sizzling.
  6. Stir the broccoli and garlic around a little bit.
  7. As the broccoli and garlic cook, add small amounts of water so they don't burn.
  8. After a few minutes (less than 5?), or when the consistency is how you like it, take the broccoli and garlic out of the pan and set aside.
  9. Add extra cooking oil to the pan if necessary.
  10. Crack in the two eggs and scramble them.
  11. When the eggs are still sort of gooey and not fully cooked, add the rice.
  12. Stir the rice and eggs around for several minutes, or until the eggs are almost cooked.
  13. Add back the broccoli and garlic, and the cooked shrimp.
  14. Add soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic powder in any amounts.
  15. Stir together for a few seconds, until the liquids are incorporated.
  16. Take everything out and put it in a bowl.
  17. Chop the green onion.
  18. Put green onion, chili oil, and salt in the food.

Notes

Before you start cooking, you should have cooked rice and cooked shrimp on hand.
